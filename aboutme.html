My Courses

Computer Networks

Acomputer networks course in computer engineering covers the fundamentals of how computers communicate and share data. Students learn about the design, configuration, and management of networks, including (LANs) and (WANs). They study protocols, data transmission, and network security. The course delves into topics like routing, switching, IP addressing, and network troubleshooting. Ultimately, it equips students with the knowledge and skills needed to build and maintain computer networks, an aspect of modern computer engineering.

Engineering Ethics

An engineering ethics course in computer engineering educates students on the moral and professional responsibilities of engineers. It covers topics like prioritizing safety, honesty, integrity, avoiding conflicts of interest, and maintaining professional competence. Students learn how to navigate ethical dilemmas and make responsible decisions in their engineering work, ensuring the well-being of the public and upholding ethical standards in the field.

Cyber Security

Acybersecurity course in computer engineering provides students with knowledge and skills to protect computer systems and data from cyber threats. It covers topics like network security, encryption, threat detection, and risk management. Students learn to defend against cyberattacks, safeguard sensitive information, and understand the evolving landscape of digital security. This course equips future computer engineers to play a vital role in ensuring the integrity and safety of digital systems and networks.

Web & IOT

AWeb & IoT course in computer engineering focuses on the technologies and concepts related to building web-based applications and connecting physical devices to the internet. Students learn web development, including programming languages like HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, along with IoT concepts such as sensor integration, data communication, and control systems. This course enables computer engineering students to bridge the gap between the digital world and physical objects for various applications and services.
